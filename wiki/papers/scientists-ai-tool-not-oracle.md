## Problem

Scientists are adopting LLMs and generative AI without adequate critical evaluation, treating AI outputs as authoritative rather than as tools requiring verification. What are the methodological risks?

## Key idea

AI does not offer a shortcut past the hard work of research. Treating AI as an oracle — a source of ground truth — leads to systematic errors: the illusion of understanding, overconfident outputs, and data leakage (the "teaching to the test" equivalent in ML-based science). Most scientific fields have "succumbed to AI hype," suspending common sense.

## Method

- Essay/opinion piece (normaltech.ai, shared widely including Hacker News discussion)
- Synthesizes examples of leakage and reproducibility failures across ML-based science
- Covers 17 disciplines with documented leakage problems
- References: growing number of papers mentioning AI (30-fold from 2010–2025)

## Results

- ML leakage pervasive across hundreds of papers in 17 disciplines
- "Illusion of understanding" is a new pitfall opened by generative AI
- Reproducibility crisis expected to worsen before improving
- AI can describe the world but not understand it — systematic error propagation

## Limitations

- Essay format without systematic empirical measurement
- Critique may overgeneralize from ML-based science to LLM-assisted research
- Does not distinguish between tool-use (data analysis, literature search) and oracle-use (generating conclusions)

## Open questions

- What verification protocols can reliably catch AI-introduced errors in scientific research?
- How should peer review adapt to assess AI-assisted methods?

## My take

A necessary corrective to uncritical AI-in-science enthusiasm. The leakage finding across 17 disciplines is alarming and empirically grounded. Most relevant for methodology sections of in-silico social science work — the oracle temptation is especially strong when AI is simulating human respondents.
