## Problem

Digital twin technology promises to model physical and social systems for prediction and control. What are the genuine potentials, and what are the ethical risks and practical limitations of applying this concept to society?

## Key idea

Digital twins (computational replicas of physical or social systems) have genuine potential for policy simulation and optimization but raise serious ethical issues: surveillance, loss of autonomy, misuse of predictive power, and the risk of optimizing for measurable proxies rather than genuine social welfare.

## Method

Book chapter reviewing digital twin technology and its applications. By Helbing and Sánchez-Vaquerizo. In Edward Elgar Handbook on Digital Twins, 2023.

## Results

- Potentials: real-time scenario planning, policy testing before implementation, early warning systems
- Ethical concerns: massive data collection required, privacy risks, predictive control creating feedback loops
- Limitations: model validity (simulations can deviate from reality), the "map is not the territory" problem, brittleness under distributional shift
- Social digital twins (modeling human behavior) face additional epistemological challenges

## Limitations

- Book chapter perspective — normative and speculative rather than empirical
- Does not engage with LLM-based simulation approaches (pre-dates the LLM agent wave)
- "Digital twin" is used loosely and may conflate engineering twins (manufacturing) with social simulation

## Open questions

- Can LLM-based generative agents make social digital twins more realistic while maintaining ethical safeguards?
- How should consent and agency be built into social digital twin systems?
- What governance structures are appropriate for government use of social digital twins?

## My take

Useful for understanding the policy application context and ethical concerns that apply to the entire in-silico social science agenda, not just digital twins. The ethical analysis (surveillance, autonomy) maps onto LLM simulation use cases as well. The "model validity" limitation is exactly what the silicon sampling validity literature is trying to address empirically.
