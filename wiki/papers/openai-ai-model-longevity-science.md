## Problem

Can an AI model trained on biological data make unexpected scientific discoveries in longevity research, specifically improving the efficacy of cellular reprogramming factors?

## Key idea

OpenAI's **GPT-4b micro** — a bespoke model trained on biological data, developed with Retro Biosciences (a longevity startup backed by Sam Altman) — suggested protein engineering modifications to two Yamanaka factors, resulting in >50x improved efficacy according to preliminary measures. This represents OpenAI's first model focused on biological data and first public claim of unexpected scientific results.

## Method

- Collaboration: OpenAI + Retro Biosciences (SF-based longevity company; $180M funding from Sam Altman)
- Task: engineer Yamanaka factors (proteins that reprogram human skin cells into stem cells)
- Model: GPT-4b micro (bespoke, not publicly released)
- Study: protein variant suggestions evaluated by Retro's lab
- Coverage: MIT Technology Review, January 17, 2025

## Results

- Two Yamanaka factors modified per AI suggestions
- >50x improvement in efficacy (by preliminary measures)
- First public OpenAI claim of unexpected scientific discovery
- Results pre-publication (companies plan to publish)

## Limitations

- Bespoke model — not reproducible or accessible to other researchers
- Results preliminary and unpublished (as of coverage date)
- "50x" figure from company — needs independent validation
- Potential conflict of interest (CEO personally funds the collaborating startup)

## Open questions

- Do the preliminary efficacy improvements translate to safe cellular reprogramming?
- Will peer-reviewed results match the preliminary 50x improvement claim?

## My take

A high-profile but caveat-laden example of AI-assisted biological discovery. The combination of company stake (Altman funding Retro) and unpublished results warrants skepticism, but if validated, a 50x improvement in Yamanaka factor efficacy would be genuinely significant for cellular reprogramming research. Signals the direction of proprietary scientific AI.
