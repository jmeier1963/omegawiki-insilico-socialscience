## Problem

European AI capabilities are limited by lack of large-scale public compute infrastructure. How should the EU build public AI compute capacity to maintain strategic autonomy and support European AI research?

## Key idea

The EU Council adopted an amendment to the EuroHPC Joint Undertaking regulation establishing "AI gigafactories" — large-scale public AI compute infrastructure across EU member states — including a quantum technologies pillar and rules for public-private partnerships.

## Method

- EU Council decision: January 16, 2026
- Available: consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/01/16/
- Regulation entered into force: January 20, 2026

## Results

- AI gigafactories established within EuroHPC JU framework
- Large-scale supercomputing infrastructure dedicated to AI training made publicly accessible to European researchers
- Quantum technologies pillar added to EuroHPC mandate
- Public-private partnership rules allow industry co-funding
- Designed to reduce Europe's dependence on US cloud providers (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) for AI compute

## Limitations

- Policy document — implementation details and timelines are separate
- Effectiveness depends on national co-investment and operational management
- Still significantly outscaled by US (OpenAI, Google, Microsoft) and Chinese government compute investments

## Open questions

- Will public AI gigafactories enable European academic AI research to compete with industry?
- How will access allocation policies work — will academic researchers have priority?

## My take

An important policy move but likely insufficient to close the Europe-US compute gap. Relevant to Ahmed et al. (2023) concerns about industrial dominance of AI: public compute is one potential remedy. The scale of EU AI gigafactories will need to grow substantially to match what US hyperscalers deploy for individual AI training runs.
