## Problem

LLMs trained on web data and fine-tuned with human feedback may encode systematic political biases. How pervasive is political bias in current LLMs, and does it skew toward particular ideological positions?

## Key idea

Reviews evidence for political bias in LLMs — particularly a left-leaning tendency in models trained with RLHF — and discusses measurement methodologies, debate about what constitutes bias, and potential mitigations.

## Results

- Multiple studies find LLMs exhibit left-leaning bias on political compass evaluations
- Bias is difficult to measure: different survey instruments yield different conclusions
- RLHF and instruction tuning amplify certain ideological tendencies relative to base models
- No consensus on whether observed bias reflects training data, annotator preferences, or measurement artifact
