## Problem

Can machine learning detect genuinely novel mathematical patterns in number-theoretic data that human mathematicians have missed?

## Key idea

In 2022, a transatlantic collaboration used ML to discover unexpected "**murmuration**" patterns in elliptic curves — oscillating structures resembling flocking birds that appear when curves are sorted by sign of functional equation. The murmuration conjecture formalizes these patterns and connects to central themes in the Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture and random matrix theory. Represents a case where AI discovered a pattern, humans formalized it.

## Method

- Discovery: ML experiments (Yang-Hui He group and collaborators) on elliptic curve data, 2022
- Formalization: arxiv 2603.09680 (2026) — "Murmurations: a case study in AI-assisted mathematics"
- The "Birch test" — a test of AI mathematical discovery — was partially passed (criteria 2 and 3 but not 1)
- Popularized by plus.maths.org article and Quanta Magazine coverage

## Results

- Murmurations: oscillating patterns in averages of elliptic curve L-function coefficients
- Connects to BSD conjecture and random matrix theory
- Partially passes the "Birch test" for AI-discovered mathematics (2 of 3 criteria)
- Represents an AI-discovered conjecture that humans could not have found easily by inspection

## Limitations

- Conjecture not yet proven
- Discovery required human interpretation to formalize
- Pattern may only hold for large families of elliptic curves

## Open questions

- Can AI discover murmurations in other L-functions or algebraic structures?
- What would full AI discovery look like under the Birch test's first criterion?

## My take

A beautiful example of AI as mathematical discovery tool: the murmuration patterns were invisible to human inspection but visible to ML.
