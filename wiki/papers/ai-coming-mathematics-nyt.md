## Problem

Are mathematicians ready for AI, and what does AI's entry into mathematics mean for the field?

## Key idea

NYT science article by Siobhan Roberts (July 2023) profiles the emerging role of AI in mathematics — particularly the **Lean theorem prover** and **SAT solvers** — asking whether AI will change how mathematicians work and what constitutes mathematical proof.

## Method

- Journalism: Siobhan Roberts, New York Times, July 2023
- Profiles researchers working on Lean (Microsoft), formal verification, and AI-assisted proof search
- Mainstream-audience accessible explainer

## Results

- Lean theorem prover enables formal verification of mathematical proofs
- AI-assisted proof search is accelerating formal mathematics
- Community debate: what counts as "understanding" in AI-assisted proof?
- Mathematicians are divided on whether AI assistance changes the nature of mathematical insight

## Limitations

- Journalism — no original research
- Written before 2025-2026 wave of Math Inc, Gemini IMO results; early coverage
- Misses the speed at which the field has since moved

## Open questions

- How has the mathematical community's attitude toward AI changed since this 2023 piece?

## My take

A historical snapshot of when the mainstream math community first seriously engaged with AI. The contrast with 2026 coverage (Gemini IMO gold medal, Math Inc Gauss) illustrates how rapidly the field has moved.
