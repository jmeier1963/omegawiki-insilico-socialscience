## Problem

Traditional surveys are static and fail to capture nuanced perspectives through follow-up. Can an LLM conduct dynamic, adaptive interviews that reveal perspectives on AI adoption at scale?

## Key idea

**Anthropic Interviewer** is an AI-powered interview tool designed to conduct qualitative research interviews at scale, adapting dynamically based on respondent answers. Initial deployment: 1,250 interviews with professionals (1,000 general workforce, 125 scientists, 125 creatives) about AI perspectives, with all data publicly released.

## Method

- AI interviewer conducts open-ended adaptive interviews
- Launched December 2025
- Initial study: 1,250 interviews across three professional cohorts
- Data publicly released for researcher access

## Results

- 86% of professionals: AI saves them time
- 65% satisfied with AI's role in their work
- 69% report social stigma around AI tool use at work
- 41% feel secure in their work (human skills irreplaceable)
- 55% express anxiety about AI's impact on their future

## Limitations

- Self-selected professional cohort; not nationally representative
- Interview questions may shape responses (demand effects)
- Publicly released data not yet subject to independent academic validation

## Open questions

- How does AI-conducted interviewing compare to human interviewing for sensitive topics?
- Can AI interviewer probe follow-up questions as effectively as skilled human interviewers?

## My take

Directly relevant to this wiki's core theme: Anthropic Interviewer is a production deployment of AI as survey/interview methodology. The public data release makes it a resource for in-silico social science research. The finding that 69% of workers feel social stigma about AI use is itself a significant substantive result for understanding AI adoption dynamics.
