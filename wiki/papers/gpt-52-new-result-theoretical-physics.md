## Problem

Can a frontier LLM independently derive a genuinely new result in theoretical physics — one not previously known in the literature?

## Key idea

OpenAI blog post describing how GPT-5.2 derived a new result in high-energy theoretical physics (single-minus gluon tree amplitudes), subsequently published as a peer-reviewed paper (arxiv 2602.12176). This represents one of the first cases of an LLM making an independently verified contribution to theoretical physics.

## Method

- OpenAI Blog, 2026
- Describes the AI-assisted derivation of the non-vanishing of single-minus gluon tree amplitudes
- Result peer-reviewed and published separately (arxiv 2602.12176)

## Results

- GPT-5.2 derived a new result in scattering amplitude theory
- Result independently verified and peer-reviewed
- Blog describes the human-AI workflow that produced the discovery

## Limitations

- Blog post; technical methodology described elsewhere (see [[single-minus-gluon-tree-amplitudes-nonzero]])
- Degree of AI autonomy vs. human guidance not fully characterized in blog format

## Open questions

- What is the replicable workflow for AI-assisted theoretical physics discovery?
- How often do such results emerge vs. AI-guided re-derivation of known results?
