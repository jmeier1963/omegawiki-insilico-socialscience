## Problem

A longstanding assumption in quantum chromodynamics held that single-minus tree-level n-gluon scattering amplitudes vanish (Parke-Taylor formalism). Are these amplitudes truly zero?

## Key idea

Single-minus gluon tree amplitudes are **nonzero** for specific "half-collinear" configurations existing in Klein space (split-signature spacetime) or for complexified momenta. Derives a closed-form piecewise-constant expression satisfying Weinberg's soft theorem and other consistency conditions.

## Method

- Theoretical derivation in high-energy physics (scattering amplitude formalism)
- Analysis in Klein space (split-signature spacetime) and complexified momenta
- Verification via Weinberg's soft theorem (consistency check)
- Authors: Guevara, Lupsasca, Skinner, Strominger, Weil

## Results

- Single-minus n-gluon amplitudes are nonzero in Klein space for half-collinear configurations
- Closed-form piecewise-constant expression derived
- Satisfies multiple consistency conditions (soft theorem, etc.)
- Revises a standard assumption in scattering amplitude theory

## Limitations

- Purely theoretical result — experimental implications are indirect
- Applies specifically to Klein space / complexified momenta configurations

## Open questions

- Can this result be extended to other helicity configurations?
