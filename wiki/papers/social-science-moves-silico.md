## Problem

How is the field of social science being transformed by in-silico methods using LLMs? What does the current landscape look like and where is the field headed?

## Key idea

Overview/perspective piece by Katharine Miller for Stanford HAI summarizing the emerging landscape of in-silico social science — using LLMs as subjects in social science research.

## Method

- Popular science / perspective article published by HAI Stanford
- No original research — synthesizes the field

## Results

- Overview of key developments, researchers, and debates
- Highlights opportunities (cost, scale, diversity) and risks (validity, representation)

## Limitations

- Not a research paper — no new empirical findings
- Popular audience framing may oversimplify methodological debates
- Blog post format means limited peer review

## Open questions

- When will in-silico methods achieve sufficient validity for mainstream social science adoption?

## My take

Useful as an accessible entry point and for tracking how the field is being represented to a broader audience. Not citable as evidence for empirical claims. Good for understanding the sociology of the field's reception.
