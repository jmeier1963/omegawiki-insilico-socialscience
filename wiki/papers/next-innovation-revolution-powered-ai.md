## Problem

How is AI transforming the innovation pipeline across industries, and what are the most impactful use cases?

## Key idea

AI is becoming a general-purpose innovation accelerator across pharmaceuticals, chemicals, materials, and knowledge work. Leading organizations are deploying AI for target identification, drug design, materials discovery, weather simulation, and research synthesis — often with dramatic speedups over prior methods.

## Method

- McKinsey QuantumBlack practitioner survey and case synthesis
- Published June 2025
- Covers pharmaceutical R&D, materials science, knowledge management, and enterprise AI adoption

## Results

- Pharmaceutical leaders deploying AI for target identification, drug candidate design, pharmacokinetic/PD analysis
- DeepMind weather forecasting: AI runs in 8 minutes vs hours on physics-based supercomputer
- ~50% of surveyed enterprises use analytical AI in innovation
- "Knowledge agents" (Google, OpenAI, Perplexity, Anthropic) perform multi-step literature synthesis
- AI surrogate models replace expensive lab screens for molecular optimization

## Limitations

- Practitioner/consultant perspective; not peer-reviewed
- Case studies cherry-picked for successful AI deployment
- Does not address failed AI deployments or cost of failed experiments

## Open questions

- What organizational changes are required to capture AI innovation benefits at scale?
- How does AI-driven innovation change the competitive landscape between firms?

## My take

A practitioner-level overview of AI-driven R&D acceleration, useful as a survey of industrial applications. Not scientifically rigorous but captures the breadth of deployment across sectors.
