## Problem

Can AI assist with genuine theoretical discovery in mathematics, and what are the implications for the future of the field?

## Key idea

AI assists mathematics through three channels: (1) **automated theorem proving**, (2) **conjecture formulation** from pattern detection, and (3) **language processing** for mathematical text. Drawing on experiments since 2017 in geometry and theoretical physics, He argues AI is already contributing to theoretical discovery, not merely computation.

## Method

- Survey/review paper based on Royal Institution lecture (submitted November 21, 2025)
- Reviews experiments in machine-learning-assisted mathematics from 2017 onward
- Covers: algebraic geometry, string theory, number theory, theoretical physics
- Demonstrates ML pattern detection across mathematical disciplines

## Results

- AI assists theorem proving (Lean/Coq framework integration)
- AI generates conjectures from data patterns in algebraic structures
- NLP/LLMs can process and generate mathematical language
- Field grown significantly since 2017 initial experiments in He's group

## Limitations

- Review paper: no new empirical results, synthesizes existing work
- AI-generated conjectures still require human verification
- Most successful applications in highly structured mathematical domains (algebra, geometry) — less so in analysis or topology

## Open questions

- Will AI-generated conjectures lead to genuinely new mathematical theories?
- What is the right human-AI collaboration model for mathematical research?
