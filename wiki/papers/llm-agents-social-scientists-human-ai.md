## Problem

The social science research pipeline — from literature review to hypothesis generation to experimental design to data analysis to writing — is labor-intensive. Can LLM agents automate or substantially assist each stage of this pipeline, and how should human researchers collaborate with such agents?

## Key idea

A human-AI collaborative platform where LLM agents assist with multiple stages of social science research: literature review automation, hypothesis generation, experimental design, data collection (via simulation or real surveys), statistical analysis, and draft writing. Researchers maintain oversight through interactive checkpoints.

## Method

Platform architecture for human-AI collaborative social science research. LLM agents handle: literature synthesis, hypothesis formulation, survey/experiment design, data simulation or collection, statistical analysis, and report generation. Human researchers review and steer at key checkpoints.

## Results

- Platform enables end-to-end social science research pipeline with LLM automation
- Demonstrates multi-stage pipeline execution on sample social science questions
- Human-AI collaboration model preserves researcher agency while reducing manual burden

## Limitations

- No systematic evaluation of output quality vs. human-only research
- Risk of LLM hallucination propagating through automated pipeline stages
- Human checkpoints may create false sense of validation if review is cursory

## Open questions

- Does the automated pipeline introduce systematic biases in hypothesis generation or analysis?
- How does output quality compare to fully human-conducted research?
- What is the optimal granularity of human checkpoints?

## My take

A useful platform contribution that addresses the practical challenge of integrating LLMs into social science workflows. The human-in-the-loop design is appropriate. The main open question is whether automated pipeline stages (especially hypothesis generation) introduce systematic biases that propagate to conclusions.
