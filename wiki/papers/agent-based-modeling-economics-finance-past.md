## Problem

How has agent-based modeling (ABM) contributed to economics and finance? What can it model that standard equilibrium methods cannot, and what are the barriers to wider adoption?

## Key idea

ABM offers economists a way to relax conventional assumptions (rational agents, equilibrium, representative agents) and study emergent social phenomena through heterogeneous interacting agents. It has yielded genuine insights in finance (clustered volatility, market impact, systemic risk) and macro/policy domains but faces adoption barriers from inference and estimation challenges.

## Method

Comprehensive review of ABM in economics and finance. Covers markets, industrial organization, labor economics, macro, development, public policy, and environmental economics. Includes a forward-looking vision for next-generation ABMs.

## Results

- ABM accomplishments include: understanding clustered volatility, market impact dynamics, systemic risk in banking networks, housing market dynamics
- Relaxing rationality and equilibrium assumptions yields qualitatively different, often more realistic dynamics
- Main limitations: lack of standard estimation/inference framework (hard to fit to data), difficulty communicating results to mainstream economists, computational cost

## Limitations

- Review perspective — doesn't present new empirical results
- Mainstream economics adoption remains limited for the methodological reasons the paper identifies
- Generative ABM (LLM-based) is not covered — this is purely classical computational ABM

## Open questions

- Can LLM-based agents replace hand-coded behavioral rules in traditional ABMs for more realistic agent cognition?
- How should ABMs be formally estimated (likelihood, moment matching, ABC methods)?
- What is the relationship between classical ABM and agent-based social simulation with LLMs?

## My take

A good reference for the pre-LLM ABM tradition. The cognitive limitations of classical hand-coded agents are exactly what LLM-based generative ABMs (Centola/Macy → AgentSociety lineage) attempt to address. Worth reading alongside the generative ABM papers for historical grounding.
