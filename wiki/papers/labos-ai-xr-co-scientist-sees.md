## Problem

Physical lab automation often requires robots to operate independently of human researchers. Can AI perceive and collaborate with humans in real-time within physical laboratory environments?

## Key idea

LabOS integrates multiple AI models, augmented reality (AR) glasses, and robotic automation to enable real-time human-machine collaboration in wet lab settings. Introduces LabSuperVision — a specialized benchmark for machine perception in scientific laboratory settings. Applications demonstrated from immunotherapy to cellular manipulation.

## Method

- Multi-model AI stack: vision (lab perception), language (instruction following), planning (experiment orchestration)
- AR glasses for human-machine interface in physical lab space
- Robotic automation for physical task execution
- LabSuperVision benchmark: evaluates AI lab perception abilities
- Case studies: immunotherapy research, cellular manipulation

## Results

- Real-time human-AI collaboration demonstrated in physical lab settings
- LabSuperVision benchmark introduced (first specialized lab perception benchmark)
- Transitions from "digital design to active lab participation" demonstrated
- Pre-print — not yet peer reviewed

## Limitations

- Pre-print without peer review
- Application scope limited to two biomedical case studies
- Robotic hardware requirements limit accessibility

## Open questions

- How does LabOS perform across different laboratory environments and experimental protocols?
- Can the XR interface scale to multi-researcher lab settings?

## My take

A forward-looking system demonstrating the potential for embodied AI lab assistants. The XR interface for human-robot collaboration is practically motivated. The LabSuperVision benchmark is a useful contribution for measuring AI perception in scientific settings. Most relevant to this wiki as an example of AI physical-world scientific automation.
