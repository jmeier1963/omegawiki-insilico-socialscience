## Problem

AI is discussed in terms of two extremes: revolutionary utopia or existential risk. Neither framing is analytically useful for governance. What is a more accurate and actionable framing?

## Key idea

Treating AI as "normal technology" — powerful and potentially transformative but not superintelligent or autonomous — provides a more accurate and actionable basis for policy. This frames AI as subject to the same institutional governance, accountability, and market forces as prior general-purpose technologies (electricity, internet).

## Method

- Essay / policy paper
- Published by Knight First Amendment Institute, Columbia University, April 2025
- Arvind Narayanan, Sayash Kapoor (Princeton)

## Results

- "Normal technology" framing: AI has impressive capabilities and real risks, but not unprecedented ones
- Governance implications: existing regulatory frameworks (consumer protection, product liability, labor law) are applicable and should be applied
- Hype critique: both utopian and existential-risk framings systematically mislead policy by focusing on hypothetical futures rather than present harms
- Accountability matters more than capability benchmarks

## Limitations

- Essay format — limited empirical support
- The "normal technology" framing may undersell genuine novelty (scale, pervasiveness, speed of deployment)
- Does not engage deeply with specific regulatory proposals

## Open questions

- How should the "normal technology" framing handle AI capabilities that genuinely exceed prior technologies (AGI scenarios)?
- Can existing frameworks handle AI-generated scientific fraud at scale?

## My take

A useful corrective to AI hype in both directions. Narayanan and Kapoor are among the most consistent voices for treating AI capability claims skeptically (see Kapoor & Narayanan 2023 on ML leakage). The "normal technology" frame is helpful for policy: it redirects attention from hypothetical superintelligence to present, tractable harms. The companion book *AI Snake Oil* (2024) develops the argument further.
