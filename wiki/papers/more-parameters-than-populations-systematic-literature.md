## Problem

The field of LLM applications in survey research is rapidly expanding but lacks a systematic mapping of how LLMs are used across the full survey process. What are the empirical applications, theoretical contributions, and open challenges across pre-data collection, data collection, and post-data collection phases?

## Key idea

Systematic PRISMA 2020 literature review: identified 1,766 candidate publications via keyword searches across arXiv, Semantic Scholar, and Web of Science. After deduplication, language filtering, and 6-year publication window (Jan 2019–Jan 2025), reviewed 965 abstracts → 161 eligible papers. Organizes LLM applications by three survey process phases.

## Method

1. PRISMA 2020 systematic review methodology
2. Search across arXiv, Semantic Scholar, Web of Science (10 LLM + 62 survey keywords)
3. 1,766 candidates → 1,099 after deduplication → 965 after exclusions → 161 eligible
4. Classify by phase: pre-data collection, data collection, post-data collection
5. Also reviewed 28 citing/cited papers outside main database → total 189 papers

## Results

Applications concentrate empirically in three areas:
- **Pre-data collection**: questionnaire writing, item generation, translation, sampling design, recruitment
- **Data collection**: AI-assisted interviews, silicon sampling, automated data collection
- **Post-data collection**: data processing, weighting, imputation, summarization, report generation, dataset curation

Key finding: silicon sampling is a focus of the data collection phase; substantial promise and pitfalls across all phases. Highlights data quality opportunities and future outlooks for LLM contribution to survey methodology.

## Limitations

- Literature window closes Jan 2025; rapidly evolving field
- Search terms and databases may miss some relevant work
- Review is descriptive rather than meta-analytic

## Open questions

- How do error and bias profiles differ across the three survey phases?
- Which LLM capabilities (language generation, reasoning, calibration) are most needed for each phase?
- What quality standards and validation protocols should apply to LLM-assisted surveys?

## My take

A useful reference for mapping the field. The three-phase framework is practical and intuitive. The systematic methodology (PRISMA, multi-database search) gives the review credibility. Main limitation is the Jan 2025 cutoff — a rapidly evolving area. Most useful as an orienting reference for the breadth of LLM applications in survey research.
