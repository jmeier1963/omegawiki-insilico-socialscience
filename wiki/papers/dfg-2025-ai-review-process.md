## Problem

The 2023 DFG prohibition on AI in peer review proved difficult to enforce, and AI tools can potentially improve review quality and efficiency. How should the DFG update its position to permit AI assistance while maintaining the integrity of the review process?

## Key idea

Reversing the 2023 prohibition, the DFG permits AI tools in a supporting capacity for reviewers, subject to four conditions designed to protect confidentiality, ensure critical engagement, and maintain human accountability.

## Method

- DFG Position paper: *Künstliche Intelligenz in der Begutachtung* (IFW 25-102, December 2025)
- Published on Zenodo: 10.5281/zenodo.18886257
- Effective date: April 16, 2026

## Results

Four conditions for permissible AI use in peer review:
1. **Data protection**: proposal content must not be permanently stored by third-party AI providers
2. **Disclosure**: reviewers must disclose their AI use and its purpose to the editor/DFG
3. **Critical review**: all AI-generated content must be critically reviewed and validated by the human reviewer
4. **Prohibition on unreflective adoption**: wholesale acceptance of AI output without independent judgment is prohibited

Reviewers remain fully responsible for the content of their reviews.

## Limitations

- Condition 1 (no permanent storage) is technically difficult to verify for cloud-based AI tools
- "Critical review" of AI-generated content is not operationalized
- Enforcement mechanisms are not specified

## Open questions

- How can DFG verify that reviewers comply with the four conditions?
- Does permitting AI in peer review improve or worsen review quality? (Empirical study needed)

## My take

A pragmatic update that acknowledges reality: AI is already being used in review (Liang et al. 2024), so prohibition without enforcement was symbolic. The four conditions are reasonable in principle. The key practical challenge is verifying compliance with condition 1 (data storage). Together with Nature Editorial (2026) and ICMJE (2023), represents the emerging governance consensus.
