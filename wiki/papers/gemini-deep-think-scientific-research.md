## Problem

Can a single frontier AI model achieve expert-level performance across diverse scientific domains — mathematics, physics, chemistry — and begin tackling genuine research-level problems?

## Key idea

**Gemini Deep Think** achieves gold-medal performance at the International Mathematics Olympiad (IMO, July 2025), gold-level results on the 2025 International Physics and Chemistry Olympiads, and is deployed to solve professional research problems in mathematics, computer science, physics, and economics. Represents a transition from competition AI to research AI.

## Method

- Google DeepMind blog announcement and companion research papers (published ~late 2025)
- Gemini Deep Think mode: extended computation at inference time
- Evaluated: IMO 2025, IPhO 2025 written section, IChO 2025 written section
- Research deployment: cross-disciplinary problems in math, CS, physics, economics

## Results

- IMO 2025: Gold medal standard
- IPhO 2025 written section: Gold medal level
- IChO 2025 written section: Gold medal level
- Research papers: demonstrates capability on professional-level problems across disciplines
- Updated version (Jan 2026) significantly outperforms original IMO-Gold version on Olympiad-level problems

## Limitations

- Olympiad performance doesn't directly translate to open-ended research discovery
- Blog announcement — peer-reviewed benchmark details limited
- Competition problems have known solutions; open research problems are fundamentally different

## Open questions

- Can Gemini Deep Think generate novel research conjectures rather than solve known problem types?
- How does Deep Think compare to human researchers on open-ended problems with no known solution?

## My take

The transition from "can AI solve competition problems" to "can AI tackle research problems" is the key inflection point this represents. The multi-discipline gold-medal sweep (math, physics, chemistry) is significant breadth.
