## Problem

Cognitive scientists rely on hand-crafted models to explain human and animal behavior. Can AI automatically discover interpretable symbolic cognitive models from behavioral data, bypassing the need for expert theory construction?

## Key idea

AI systems can discover symbolic (interpretable, human-readable) cognitive models from human and animal behavioral data by combining program synthesis, reinforcement learning, and symbolic regression. The discovered models are both predictive and interpretable — matching or improving on expert-designed models.

## Method

- Program synthesis + symbolic regression applied to behavioral time-series data
- Reinforcement learning as the behavioral framework (action-reward data)
- Discovery of symbolic expressions that explain behavior patterns
- Evaluated against human and animal behavioral datasets (tasks from cognitive psychology and neuroscience)
- Key authors include researchers from Google DeepMind and Princeton (Daw, Miller, Stachenfeld)

## Results

- AI discovers symbolic cognitive models that match/exceed expert-designed models in predictive accuracy
- Discovered models are interpretable (symbolic expressions, not black boxes)
- Applicable to both human and animal behavioral data across multiple task types
- Approach generalizes across reinforcement learning environments

## Limitations

- Behavioral data from controlled lab tasks; generalization to naturalistic behavior unclear
- Symbolic models may be overfit to specific task structures
- Pre-print — not yet peer reviewed

## Open questions

- Do AI-discovered cognitive models reveal genuinely new psychological insights?
- How do these symbolic models compare to the neural cognitive models (Centaur) approach?

## My take

Highly relevant to this wiki's broader interest in AI models of human cognition. While the existing Centaur foundation model takes a black-box neural approach to predicting human behavior, this paper takes the complementary symbolic/interpretable route. The tension between predictive power and interpretability is a live debate in computational cognitive science.
