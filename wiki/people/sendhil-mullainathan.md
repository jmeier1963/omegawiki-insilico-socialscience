## My notes

Leading behavioral economist and computer scientist, Harvard PhD, now at UChicago Booth. Co-author of "Scarcity: Why Having Too Little Means So Much" (2013). Widely cited for applying ML methods to social science problems, including causal inference and algorithmic fairness. The QJE 2024 paper with Ludwig is particularly relevant as a demonstration that ML can generate novel hypotheses beyond what human researchers would propose — a key methodological contribution for AI-driven social science.
