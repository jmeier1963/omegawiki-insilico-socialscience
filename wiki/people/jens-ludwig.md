Director of the UChicago Crime Lab and one of the leading empirical economists studying crime, poverty, and policy. Co-authored the influential QJE 2024 paper with Mullainathan demonstrating that ML-generated hypotheses can reveal patterns in social data that theory-driven researchers would not have proposed — a methodologically significant contribution at the intersection of ML and causal social science.
