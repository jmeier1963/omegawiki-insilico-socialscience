## Statement

While AI-engaged research has grown 13-fold across scientific disciplines (1985–2022), AI publications and traditional non-AI publications in the same fields remain semantically distant — they spread across venues (ubiquity) but do not integrate conceptually (no mixing), producing an "oil-and-water" pattern.

## Evidence summary

Duede et al. (2024): bibliometric analysis of ~80M publications, document embeddings confirm systematic semantic tension in all 20 fields studied.

## Conditions and scope

- 20 scientific fields, 1985-2022 dataset
- LLM era post-2022 not covered — integration patterns may have shifted
- Semantic distance via embedding may not capture actual conceptual overlap

## Counter-evidence

- No counter-evidence in the literature yet; post-2022 anecdotes of AI-biology collaboration exist but are not systematically studied

## Open questions
