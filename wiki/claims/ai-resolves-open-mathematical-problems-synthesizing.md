## Statement

AI can resolve a meaningful number of "open" mathematical problems by synthesizing knowledge across disciplinary boundaries and identifying existing solutions scattered across the literature — many problems remain open due to knowledge fragmentation rather than intrinsic difficulty.

## Evidence summary

Feng et al. (2026): Gemini evaluates 700 Erdős conjectures; resolves 13 (5 novel, 8 literature identifications). Main insight: knowledge silos, not mathematical hardness, explain many open problems.

## Conditions and scope

- Erdős Problems database specifically; may not generalize to harder open problems
- Human expert panel required for validation
- "Resolution" includes literature identification, not only new proofs

## Counter-evidence

- 13/700 = ~1.9% resolution rate; most open problems remain open even with AI assistance
- Risk of "subconscious plagiarism" — AI solutions may inadvertently reproduce unattributed existing work

## Open questions
