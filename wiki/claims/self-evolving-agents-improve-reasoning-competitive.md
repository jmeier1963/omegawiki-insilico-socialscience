## Statement

Dual-agent competitive curriculum learning — where a curriculum agent and learner agent co-evolve from the same base model without human-annotated data — yields significant improvements on mathematical and general reasoning benchmarks.

## Evidence summary

Xia et al. (2025): +18% math, +24% general reasoning on benchmarks for Qwen3-8B-Base via Agent0 self-evolution framework.

## Conditions and scope

- Qwen3-8B-Base; results may vary across model scales and architectures
- Benchmark improvements may reflect overfitting to task types generated by the curriculum agent
- Evaluated on standard reasoning benchmarks, not novel or out-of-distribution tasks

## Counter-evidence

- No direct comparison to models trained on equivalently scaled human-curated data
- Self-training methods often suffer from reward hacking or distribution drift in longer runs

## Linked ideas

## Open questions

- Does performance improvement continue with more self-evolution iterations or does it plateau?
- Does self-evolution improve genuinely novel problem-solving or primarily in-distribution benchmark performance?
