## Statement

LLM agents can assist with or automate each stage of the social science research pipeline — including literature synthesis, hypothesis generation, experimental design, data analysis, and report generation — when integrated in a human-AI collaborative platform with appropriate oversight checkpoints.

## Evidence summary

LLM Agents as Social Scientists (2604.01520): platform architecture enabling multi-stage pipeline automation; demonstrates execution on sample research questions; human researchers maintain oversight through interactive checkpoints.

## Conditions and scope

- Applies to platform-assisted automation, not fully autonomous research
- No systematic quality evaluation vs. human-only research
- Risk of hallucination propagating through pipeline stages

## Counter-evidence

- No empirical comparison of output quality vs. human research
- Automation may introduce systematic biases (especially in hypothesis generation)
- Human oversight requirement limits true automation benefit

## Open questions

- How does automated pipeline output quality compare to human-conducted research?
- Which pipeline stages benefit most from LLM automation?
- What types of social science questions are most and least amenable to automation?
