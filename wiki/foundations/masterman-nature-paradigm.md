## Definition

Masterman's 1970 essay (in Lakatos & Musgrave, *Criticism and the Growth of Knowledge*) conducts a close reading of Kuhn's *Structure* and identifies at least 21 distinct senses in which Kuhn uses "paradigm," grouping them into three types: metaphysical, sociological, and artifact/construct paradigms.

## Intuition

Kuhn's text is internally inconsistent about what a paradigm is. But this ambiguity is theoretically productive: the most scientifically useful sense is the "paradigm as puzzle-solving artifact" — a concrete exemplar (calculation, instrument, experimental result) that guides research without needing explicit codification in rules.

## Formal notation

No formal notation. Three paradigm types:
- **Metaphysical paradigm**: a set of beliefs/worldview
- **Sociological paradigm**: a universally recognized scientific achievement
- **Artifact paradigm**: a concrete puzzle-solving tool (the most fruitful sense)

## Key variants

- **Kuhn (1969 Postscript)**: adopts Masterman's clarification; distinguishes "disciplinary matrix" (sociological) from "exemplar" (artifact)

## Known limitations

- Conceptual analysis of another thinker's text; limited independent theoretical contribution
- The 21-sense analysis may overcorrect toward hyper-precision

## Open problems

- Which "paradigm" sense is most useful for describing AI-driven science (Hey 2009)?

## Relevance to active research

Essential companion to Kuhn for precision in STS discourse. Useful when evaluating whether AI-driven methods constitute a new "paradigm" in Masterman's artifact sense — i.e., do LLMs provide a new class of concrete puzzle-solving exemplars?
