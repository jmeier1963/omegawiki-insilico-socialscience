## Definition

Hey, Tansley, and Tolle's 2009 Microsoft Research anthology (open access) expands Jim Gray's vision of a fourth scientific paradigm — following empirical (observation), theoretical (models), and computational (simulation) science — in which the analysis of massive datasets becomes the primary driver of discovery.

## Intuition

Jim Gray argued shortly before his death that science was entering a new era: not just simulating nature (third paradigm) but drowning in data and mining it for patterns. The fourth paradigm requires new tools, infrastructure, and epistemic practices — and a new kind of scientist comfortable with data wrangling, machine learning, and cross-disciplinary borrowing.

## Formal notation

Four paradigms of science:
1. **Empirical** (thousands of years): observation and experiment recorded in natural history
2. **Theoretical** (~last few centuries): models, laws, generalizations
3. **Computational** (~last few decades): simulation of complex phenomena
4. **Data-intensive** (eScience): capture, curate, analyze large datasets using ML tools

## Key variants

- **Anderson (2008)**: more radical claim — theory itself is obsolete (contested)
- **Chai et al. (2024)**: fifth paradigm — autonomous "robot scientist"
- **FAIR principles** (Wilkinson 2016): infrastructure for the fourth paradigm

## Known limitations

- "Paradigm" is used loosely (Masterman would find many senses here too)
- The fourth paradigm has not displaced the first three — they coexist
- Underestimates how much theoretical scaffolding data analysis requires

## Open problems

- Is AI-driven autonomous research a fifth paradigm (robot scientist), or is it within the fourth?
- What infrastructure does the fifth paradigm require beyond FAIR data?

## Relevance to active research

Foundational framing for the AI-in-science literature. All AI-science papers (Boiko 2023, Szymanski 2023, Jumper 2021, etc.) are developments within or extensions of the fourth paradigm. The Chai et al. (2024) "robot scientist" is the proposed fifth paradigm.
