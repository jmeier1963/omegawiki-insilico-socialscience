## Definition

Longino's 1990 monograph argues for "contextual empiricism": scientific hypotheses are always underdetermined by data, so background assumptions (which may encode social values) inevitably shape inquiry. Objectivity is not achieved individually but through communal practices of public criticism by diverse perspectives.

## Intuition

There is no purely neutral vantage point in science — background assumptions always mediate between data and hypothesis. Rather than eliminating values, we should institute social practices (venues for criticism, uptake of criticism, shared standards, tempered equality of intellectual authority) that expose and check value-laden assumptions.

## Formal notation

No formal notation. Four conditions for objectivity through social epistemology:
1. **Venues for criticism**: public forums for challenging evidence and methods
2. **Uptake of criticism**: community must actually respond to critique
3. **Public standards**: explicitly shared evaluative criteria
4. **Tempered equality**: diversity of perspectives with equal authority

## Key variants

- **Longino (2002)**: *The Fate of Knowledge* — extends and revises
- **Solomon (2001)**: social empiricism
- **Miriam Solomon**: voting-based model of scientific consensus

## Known limitations

- The four conditions are idealizations rarely met in practice
- May underspecify how to adjudicate between competing value-laden frameworks
- Tension between diversity requirement and actual operation of disciplinary authority

## Open problems

- How does AI-generated research affect the social epistemology of science? Does AI participation in research erode the diversity of perspectives?
- Ahmed et al. (2023) on industrial dominance of AI research is directly relevant.

## Relevance to active research

Provides normative framework for evaluating AI's effect on scientific diversity. Ahmed et al. (2023) on industry concentration, and Messeri & Crockett (2024) on AI monocultures, both raise Longinoan concerns about whether the social conditions for objectivity are being met.
